<h1 id="Research">Research</h1>
I like to view astrophysical problems from both perspectives of physics and statistics in survey data. I am interested in observational cosmology and astrophysics.

## Scattering transform: new statistics for cosmology and astrophysics
Using a new statistical tool called the _scattering transform_ to analyse the [density field of the universe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Observable_universe#Large-scale_structure) through its [weak lensing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weak_gravitational_lensing) effect, we may extract more cosmological information than traditional methods and on a par with state-of-the-art CNNs. The scattering transform, which non-linearly transforms the original field and yields a compact set of robust and interpretable statistics, can be used as a general tool for non-Gaussian information extraction. ## Discoveries about white dwarfs from _Gaia_ satellite
I also work on several projects about [white dwarfs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_dwarf), the destiny of most stars in the universe. Using data from the [_Gaia_](https://www.cosmos.esa.int/web/gaia/home) space mission, I revealed that some white dwarfs cool extremely slowly and that some are merger products. My work has led to two papers (click the figures below), one of which was highlighted by [astrobites](https://astrobites.org/2019/11/12/the-slowly-cooling-white-dwarfs-who-say-ne/) and [AAS Nova](https://aasnova.org/2019/11/19/the-slowly-cooling-white-dwarfs-who-say-ne/).
